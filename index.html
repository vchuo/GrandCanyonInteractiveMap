Welcome
What you will find here is an interactive map of the Grand Canyon, developed as a part of a parent project in a digital humanities lab to serve as a portal for readers to explore a slideshow of 41 landscape photographs of the Grand Canyon taken by Henry Peabody (a prominent commercial photographer during his time) circa 1905 and the essays written about these photographs for the parent project, all while spatially orienting themselves on a map of the Grand Canyon.
This interactive map features two exploration modes (which you can activate using the One and All buttons situated at the bottom-left of your window), two layers (which you can toggle on/off using the checkboxes also situated at the bottom-left of your window), zoom functionality (via mousewheel or the zoom buttons situated at the top-right of your window) and it is draggable. To learn more about the exploration modes/layers, hover your cursor over their respective buttons/checkboxes.

Introduction
Explore By Station Point: Which parts of the Grand Canyon can I see in this one photo?
Examine the viewsheds and photos of the 41 station points individually by hovering your cursor over a station point; some of the station points do not have viewsheds associated with them because what can be seen in the photo associated with the station point is too limited when displayed on this map (i.e. station points 14, 15 and 16). Click on any station point to enlarge its photo, and click on this larger photo to view its associated photo module.
Note: there are no station points 1 and 36.
Terminology:
Station Point: The location believed to be where Peabody took a respective photo; depicted as a numbered, red-colored circle on this map.
Viewshed: Areas of the map visible from a photo (station point); depicted as red-shaded areas on this map.
Explore By Location: Show me all the photos where the location I am pointing to is visible?
Move your cursor around the map to see which locations are visible from the photos taken by Henry Peabody, and click on any location that is visible from at least one photo to get links to the individual web modules created for these photos. If a location is visible from a photo, the viewshed of the photo will be displayed and the corresponding station point will be highlighted.
